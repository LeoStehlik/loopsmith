# Research Brief Quality Pack

Loopsmith can be used to improve research brief quality, not just direct-chat replies or proof-heavy engineering outputs.

This pack pattern is for cases where a research agent is technically competent but operationally weak to read: repetitive, over-structured, over-complete, and too willing to keep weak topics alive.

## Common failure modes

A research brief quality pack should usually look for problems like:
- repeated thesis inflation across sections
- template fatigue where every topic gets the same shape
- weak-topic retention instead of drop or merge discipline
- reader-specific scaffolding repeated too often
- completeness theatre instead of usefulness
- low scan value in the opening screen or first minute

## What a stronger candidate policy should do

A good candidate policy often pushes toward:
- ranking topics before drafting
- leading with the 1 to 3 strongest developments immediately
- hard limits on substantive section count
- merging sections that point to the same underlying story
- dropping topics that only weakly reconfirm an already-known thesis
- allowing brief tracker notes instead of full writeups
- rewarding signal density over broad coverage

## Example policy shape

A compact candidate policy for a research brief agent might say:

- write as if the operator will only read the first minute
- maximum 4 substantive sections
- if two sections tell the same story, combine them
- weak topics become tracker notes or disappear
- do not repeat reader-specific implications blocks by default
- if the day is thin, say so plainly and keep the brief short

## What to evaluate

The evaluator does not need to solve taste perfectly. A useful first pass can simply test whether the candidate policy structurally fights the known bad habits.

Helpful checks include:
- does it rank or explicitly select strongest items first?
- does it permit dropping or merging weak topics?
- does it constrain scope?
- does it ban repeated scaffolding?
- does it allow thin-day honesty?
- does it prefer signal density over category completion?

## Why this matters

Many research agents fail in the same boring way: they become diligent summary machines instead of selective scouts.

A research brief quality pack makes that failure mode explicit, testable, and improvable.
